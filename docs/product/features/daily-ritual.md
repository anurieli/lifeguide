# Daily Ritual — the Morning Scroll and the Night Scroll

**Status:** built (v5, typed components — inline mantra + settings-driven morning journal + daily-quote tidbit) · **Element of:** the Sessions stream (the two calm bookends of the day) · **Owns:** `ritualItems`, `ritualDays`, `roadmapEntries`, `morningNotes`

> The morning is an ordered primer sequence, not a checklist: read the note last-night you left, read the doctrine, take in the day's mantra, walk the roadmap set last night, answer the morning journal. The evening's real job is setting tomorrow's morning up — the roadmap plus a note to morning-you. Permanent practices ride alongside on the rituals rail. Seal it, sleep, repeat.
>
> **Naming (Ariel, 2026-07-13):** the two bookends are branded the **Morning Scroll** and the **Night Scroll** — that is what the tabs and the sequence-card headers say. "Ritual" remains the internal/system term (tables, code, the rituals rail); "scroll" is the face the person sees.

## 1. The philosophy (governs every design choice here)

1. **The morning ritual is an ORDERED PRIMER SEQUENCE, not a checklist.** The person walks it top to bottom and it primes the mind for the day: the current step is quietly held, the walked steps settle. Plain practice-checking exists *alongside* (the **rituals rail**), never as the spine. They are called **rituals**, never to-dos (Ariel, 2026-07-12).
2. **The evening ritual's real job is building TOMORROW MORNING'S ROADMAP.** Before bed, the person sets exactly what tomorrow starts with by **picking from the goals they already track**, not by typing a second to-do list (ARI-144, Ariel: "I'm not adding to-do items; I should be able to pick from the to-do list"). Each pick is a **pointer** at an open canonical `goalTasks` row, so the roadmap is a selection over real work, never a duplicate list. The next morning opens with that roadmap as its ordered spine: wake up executing, not deciding. Entries target the NEXT ritual day across the 4am boundary ([ADR 0012](../../decisions/0012-roadmap-targets-next-ritual-day.md)): set at 23:00 or at 1:30am, both land on the upcoming morning. New tasks are still created on the [Goals board](goals.md), never in the scroll; the scroll only pulls from what is already there. **Legacy free-form entries** (written before ARI-144) keep rendering and behaving exactly as before.
   The whole night step is framed as **leaving something for morning-you** — and alongside the structured roadmap the person can leave one free-form **note to morning-you** (marked ✳): a reminder, a warning, a word. It rides the same target-day convention (`morningNotes`, one note per morning, rewritable all evening, empty tears it up), and the next morning's scroll **opens with it** — the last thing written at night is the first thing read in the morning.
3. **Ritual steps are TYPED SCHEMA COMPONENTS** ([ADR 0011](../../decisions/0011-typed-ritual-components.md)). One ritual = an ordered list of typed components. Kinds today: `do` (a permanent practice, on the rituals rail; assignable to morning, night, or **any** — indifferent to the time of day), `read` (a *long* readout behind the immersive reader — inline words or the Blueprint), `mantra` (a *short* line shown **inline** — no reader, no Read tap — fixed or drawn from the rotating pool, differing by the day), `question` (a reflection prompt: fixed words, or — content-less in the morning — the **settings-driven journal**; content-less at night — the rotating evening bank), `roadmap` (the evening builder / the morning display), `tidbit` (a **daily inspirational quote** shown inline, generated by the cheap Haiku [daily-quote agent](daily-tidbit.md) — or a fixed line the person pins). Future kinds slot in by widening a union — no migration.
   **Read vs mantra (Ariel, 2026-07-15):** the two are deliberately split. A mantra is short — it belongs *right there* on the step, absorbed in a breath, no button between the person and the words; it rotates so the line differs by the day. The Read button is reserved for the *big* thing — the Blueprint — where an immersive full-screen read earns the tap.
4. **The Core is the person (character, who you are); the Blueprint is the conduct doctrine (how the day is lived).** The Today page serves the doctrine layer. The two interlink (the read step resolves from [the Blueprint](the-blueprint.md)) but never merge.

No scores, no guilt: an unfinished day simply passes; a finished one gets one quiet golden moment. The one deliberate carve-out is the **gentle keeping-up run** ([ADR 0018](../../decisions/0018-gentle-keeping-up-run.md)) — a penalty-free count of how many days in a row both bookends were sealed, shown quietly beside the keeping-up strip. It never shames a miss (a gap just resets the run), never keeps a longest-ever high score, and stays silent at zero. Streaks *as an engine* — meters, streak-loss states, notifications, points — remain rejected.

## 2. User-facing behavior

**Only the beat you are in is reachable** (Ariel, 2026-07-12). The Morning/Evening control is not a free toggle — it is **locked to the clock**. From 4:00 to 16:59 the morning is live and the evening tab is locked (a padlock, muted, unclickable, with the hint "The night ritual opens at 5:00 PM"); from 17:00 through 3:59 it flips — the morning locks with "opens at 4:00 AM". At 17:00 you can no longer open the morning; at 4:00 you can no longer reopen last night. You only ever act on the ritual you are actually in. The locked ritual's contents are not rendered at all — there is nothing to peek at or edit out of its window. (The cutoffs are the same `NIGHT_START_HOUR` / `DAY_ROLLOVER_HOUR` as everything else in `lib/ritual.ts`; the lock is computed once per mount, so crossing a cutoff with the page open just needs a revisit.) Every submission inside a beat — each question answer, each roadmap walk, each seal — is still recorded to `interactions` and reflected in the rituals rail's keeping-up strip, so the day's record is complete regardless of which beat is currently open.

**Morning.** Today lands on the Morning Scroll tab (cutoffs in `lib/ritual.ts`). If last-night you left a note, the scroll **opens with it**: a gold-washed "✳ From last-night you" card above the sequence, read-only. Below it, the sequence card walks its components in order, typically: **Read the Blueprint** (tap Read → the [immersive reader](#the-immersive-reader) fills the screen, reaching the end marks it read), **Read the mantra** (the day's mantra shown right there, inline; nothing to tap open, and the line itself is **editable in place** (see below); the circle is the acknowledgment), **Walk today's roadmap** (the tasks pulled last night, each tapped done as it happens; a linked task's check flows through to the Goals board; walking the last one checks the step), **The morning journal** (a write-in-place reflection whose prompt follows the person's **Daily Exercise** setting — Intention / Gratitude / Free — and is **retunable right in the scroll**; answered typed or spoken — the daytime mirror of the night's Check out), and **Today's quote** (an AI-generated line drawn from the person's Core and cached for the day; see [daily-tidbit.md](daily-tidbit.md)). The first unwalked step carries a faint gold wash — the sequence has a "you are here." Plain practices (drink the water) sit on the **rituals rail**: a right-hand panel on desktop, a card under the sequence on the phone. When every component AND every morning-assigned rail practice is done, the scroll card turns gold.

**The header count reflects only the scroll (ARI-133).** The small "X/Y" beside the scroll's label counts **only the rendered scroll cards**: the non-`do` spine (read, mantra, question, roadmap, tidbit). A `do`-rail practice is not a scroll card, so it never moves either side of that bar: a morning with five cards on screen reads out of five, never out of seven because two rail practices ride alongside. Completion is a **separate, wider** bar: the gold state and the **seal** still require *every* item of the ritual, the assigned rail practices included (`isRitualComplete` over all of `mine`; the header uses `scrollProgress` over the spine). A rail-only bookend (no scroll cards) shows no count at all rather than "0/0", because its practices live entirely on the rail. The **Seal the morning** act itself no longer sits at the foot of that card; it renders as a separate element (`RitualSeal`) at the very bottom of Today, **below the Horizons ladder** (reordered 2026-07-20), so the day's spine reads top to bottom: walk the scroll → set your horizons → seal. The seal only appears once every step is checked; before that its slot is empty.

**The mantra step (Ariel, 2026-07-15; editable in place, ARI-144).** A `mantra` component renders its line **inline**, in place — no Read button, because a mantra is short and read in a breath. Its words are the person's own fixed line, or (left empty) one drawn from the rotating **mantra pool** (`lib/mantras.ts`, seeded from the [Coach KB mantra pool](../coach-knowledge-base/mantras.md)); the pool rotates deterministically per ritual day, so the line **differs by the day** and is stable across devices until the 4am rollover. Reading it and tapping the circle checks the step. **In the Morning Scroll the line is directly editable right where it is read**: a calm pencil affordance appears on hover, and tapping the words opens a field seeded with today's resolved mantra (the fixed line, or the day's pool line). Saving replaces the pool line with the person's own words (writing `content` via `ritualItems.updateItem`), so it becomes the **fixed mantra going forward**; blank or unchanged keeps the pool. Editing the text never toggles the acknowledgment circle. This in-place editor is scoped to the **morning** (that was the request); a Night Scroll mantra renders read-only here and is still edited through the shared **Edit** mode. (The Read button stays exclusive to the long `read` — the Blueprint.) **Exactly one mantra:** there is never a second mantra beside a legacy behind-the-button "Read the mantra" read — the v4 reconcile (below) folds any such read into the single inline `mantra`, keeping the person's own words.

**The morning journal — settings-driven, retunable in place (Ariel, 2026-07-15).** The morning's write-in-place reflection is the point of the morning: the thing a Coach most wants to see — where the person's head is as the day starts. Its prompt is **not** the generic rotating bank; it follows the person's **Daily Exercise** setting (Settings → "What the check-in asks you"; `settings.dailyExercise` = `intention` | `gratitude` | `free`) via `journalPromptFor` (`lib/questions.ts`): *"What's your intention for today?"* / *"What are you grateful for this morning?"* / *"How are you, really, this morning? Say whatever's there."* And it is **editable right in the scroll** — a small **Prompt · Intention / Gratitude / Free** picker sits under the question and writes `settings.dailyExercise`, so the day's prompt re-resolves live without a trip to Settings. Mechanically it is a content-less `question` in the morning ritual; a morning question with its own fixed words overrides the setting (and hides the picker). The night's Check out is unchanged — it still rotates the evening bank. Answers publish `ritual_question` to the Bus exactly as before, so the journal feeds the Mirror/Coach.

**Where the journal is going ([ADR 0030](../../decisions/0030-the-journal-is-the-cores-incremental-intake.md), accepted, unbuilt).** The morning journal is also meant to be **the Core's main intake**: alongside the Daily Exercise prompt, one thing the Core still doesn't know surfaces each day, and answering it writes to the Core's own `coreResponses` keys — so the foundation gets built by journaling rather than by an hour-long sit-down at the Core. The likely mechanism is a new `core` kind on the `ritualItems.kind` union (union-widening per ADR 0011, no migration). Rules that bind the implementation — one a day, never re-ask a settled question, one store, his words win, always skippable, absent when the Blueprint is complete — live in the ADR. Tracked by [ARI-120](https://linear.app/cuttheedge/issue/ARI-120).

**The rituals rail** (reworked 2026-07-12 per Ariel's feedback — no morning/night sections, no double add box): ONE flat list of the person's permanent practices, each row carrying a **time marker** instead of a section — sun (morning), moon (night), sun-moon (**anytime**, indifferent to the time of day). Practices are permanent to the profile and checked fresh each day; morning/night ones count toward their bookend's seal, anytime ones belong to neither seal. Under the subtitle a quiet **reset countdown** ("↻ Resets in 6h 12m", Ariel 2026-07-15) says how long the day's checks still stand — it ticks each minute toward the next 4am rollover (`msUntilRollover` / `formatCountdown` in `lib/ritual.ts`), the same boundary every ritual day resets on. Hovering a row reveals a **✕**; deleting always warns first — "Delete this ritual? It'll be removed from all rituals — every day, not just today." — because a delete removes the practice from the profile, not from one day. **One add affordance at the bottom**, closable: open it, type the practice, and pick morning / night / anytime on the segmented picker; enter adds and keeps the form open for the next one. At the **top of the card** sits the **keeping-up calendar** (moved up and reworked from the old bottom strip, Ariel 2026-07-15): a minimalist last-14-days row — one pip per day above its date-of-month number — that reads at a glance in **three states**: a **solid gold** pip once both bookends sealed (a *finished*, kept day — the same bar the run counts), a **soft gold ring** once the day is merely *started* (anything ticked but not yet sealed — so a begun-but-unsealed day is finally visible, the gap Ariel flagged), and a **bare outline** when untouched; today's date reads gold. Beside the "Keeping up" label sits the **gentle run** ([ADR 0018](../../decisions/0018-gentle-keeping-up-run.md)) — "N in a row," penalty-free, hidden at zero. It reads `rituals.history` (now per-day `checked` counts + `completedAt` across morning / night / any, so *started* and *finished* are both derivable). The morning/night dot split of the old strip is folded into the single per-day pip; no score, no shaming.

**Evening.** The Night Scroll tab centers on two components: **Check out** (a reflection question drawn from a rotating bank — a different honest question each night) and **Set tomorrow's roadmap** (**"Pull from your goals"**: a picker over the person's open Goals tasks, labelled with its goal, tapped to add; optional "+ where / info" per entry, seeded from the task description; reorder with arrows; ✕ removes it from the roadmap, never deletes the task). A quiet count confirms "3 things set for tomorrow morning." Under the builder, behind a dashed rule, sits **"✳ A note for morning-you — it opens tomorrow's scroll"**: one free-form field, saved on blur, rewritable all evening; leaving it empty (or emptying it) means no note. After the seal it shows read-only as "✳ Left for the morning." **Close the day** seals it.

**The roadmap picks canonical Goals tasks (ARI-144).** A roadmap entry is a **pointer** at an open `goalTasks` row, not an independent to-do. The picker (`roadmap.availableTasks`) offers the person's own **open** tasks (unchecked, not "waiting"), minus any already on that day's roadmap, so the same task cannot be picked twice; the mutation (`roadmap.addFromTask`) enforces that same eligibility server-side (a checked or waiting task is rejected, not merely hidden). Each entry stores `goalTaskId` plus a **snapshot** of the task content at pick time. The morning display resolves the task's **current** content, so a rename on the Goals board shows through; a linked entry's **done state follows the canonical task alone** (a completion or reopen on the Goals board is reflected, and a stale local mark never wins). Checking a linked entry off in the morning **mirrors through**: it checks the canonical `goalTasks` row and preserves the existing Todoist push (`todoist.pushChecked`). If the linked task is later **deleted**, the entry falls back to its stored snapshot text and behaves like a plain legacy entry (its own `doneAt`).

**Everything is his.** Edit (top-right) manages every component of that ritual in one place: rename, reorder, delete, rewrite a fixed mantra line / inline read words / a fixed question, add any kind (+ ritual, + mantra, + something to read, + question, + roadmap, + read from the Blueprint). A mantra or a question left without fixed words rotates through its pool/bank; the edit-mode hint shows today's line. A brand-new user is seeded the default set below; deleting everything leaves it deleted.

**Defaults (new accounts):** morning — Read the Blueprint (from [the Blueprint document](the-blueprint.md)), Read the mantra (rotating mantra pool), Today's quote ([daily-quote agent](daily-tidbit.md)), Drink a glass of water (rail), Walk today's roadmap, The morning journal (settings-driven question). Night — Check out (rotating question), Set tomorrow's roadmap.

**Existing accounts** are brought current exactly once (`rituals.upgradeToSeedVersion`, marked by `settings.ritualsSeedVersion`, now at **v5**), in two moves: **(a) add** any missing kinds (mantra, the daily-quote **tidbit**, roadmap, journal question) after existing items, only into rituals that still have items, deletable forever — but never seed a second mantra beside a legacy read-mantra; and **(b) reconcile** the v3 fallout — collapse every mantra-ish morning item (a legacy behind-the-button "Read the mantra" read AND the v3-seeded inline mantra) down to **one inline `mantra`**, keeping the item that carries the person's own words, and **clear the stale fixed "one move" morning question** (`"What's one small thing today that points at it?"`) so it becomes the settings-driven journal. v4 is a deliberate one-shot repair of a duplicate v3 itself created (ADR 0011 is otherwise additive-only); it touches only the exact stale default string and the mantra duplicates, and runs once. The Blueprint read is adopted explicitly, per ritual — the **read from the Blueprint** button in edit mode (shown on morning and night alike, whichever one still lacks it), or the Settings card's "Read it each morning" for the morning specifically. `rituals.adoptBlueprintRead({ ritual })` is idempotent per ritual: morning and night each get their own blueprint-sourced read step, adopted and checked off independently, both resolving live from the same Blueprint document.

### The immersive reader

Tapping a read step opens a full-screen, in-page overlay ([ADR 0013](../../decisions/0013-immersive-reader-overlay.md)): the page holds still, the words scroll inside — one measure, generous line height, the doctrine rendered pillar by pillar. Reaching the end marks the step read with a "Read ✓" confirmation and reveals a pinned **red "Done" button** — it does **not** auto-close (revised 2026-07-20; the original design released 1.1s after the bottom). Only tapping that button, or the always-visible header close, dismisses the overlay; closing early via the header X marks nothing. Short texts count as read after a considered pause, same rule: the button still has to be tapped. "Read again" is always offered. The Blueprint's own full-screen structured view (`components/settings/BlueprintImmersive.tsx`, opened from Settings, not from this reader) shares the same overlay/release chrome — see [the-blueprint.md](the-blueprint.md).

## 4. Dynamics and interactions with other elements

- **Hosted by [Home (Today)](dashboard.md):** the sequence card renders inside the toggled beat; the rituals rail is the page's right-hand panel (desktop) or a card below (phone). The Dashboard stays draw-only.
- **Reads from [the Blueprint](the-blueprint.md):** a `read` step with `source: "blueprint"` resolves its words live from the person's Blueprint document — no duplicated text; a Settings edit tonight is tomorrow's read.
- **Publishes to the Sessions stream:** `ritual_completed` on seal and `ritual_question` on every answer land in `interactions`, so the Mirror/Coach fold conduct and reflection into context, and the [Today log](dashboard.md) renders the day's journal from them.
- **The roadmap loop is self-contained:** tonight's builder and tomorrow's spine are the same `roadmapEntries` rows under one target-day key (ADR 0012) — no handoff, no copy.
- **Sibling of the [Journal](journal.md):** the Journal's adaptive beats and this element share the bookend moments; neither owns the other. The question bank is the fixed-prompt cousin of the Journal's adaptive prompts.
- **Seeded by the doctrine** ([`../../research/blueprint-for-living.md`](../../research/blueprint-for-living.md)) — via the Blueprint document's seed and the default components.

## 5. States

- **Unseeded / fresh day / in progress / all-checked / sealed / empty ritual / editing:** as v1 (empty day rows are created on first check; gold at all-checked; `completedAt` locks checks; delete-all invites, never re-seeds).
- **Beat locked (the off-hours ritual):** the non-current tab is a padlock, muted and unclickable, with an "opens at HH:MM" hint; its card is not rendered. Morning locks at 17:00, night at 4:00.
- **Sequence position:** the first unchecked spine component carries the "current" wash; sealed or fully-walked rituals have none.
- **Question answered:** the field stays live, seeded with the saved answer; it auto-saves on blur (and after a voice take). Re-editing then clicking out logs a new event (history keeps both, the step shows the latest); an empty or unchanged blur logs nothing.
- **Roadmap, morning, nothing set:** "What does today start with? Pull from your goals below." — the picker is right there. (No mention of last night when nothing was left — an empty roadmap is irrelevant history, so the copy just points at today. Ariel, 2026-07-15.)
- **Roadmap, morning, set last night:** "Left by last-night you. Walk it, top to bottom." — the reminder of what last-night you set.
- **Roadmap picker, no open tasks:** "No open tasks to pull. Add tasks on the Goals board, then set your roadmap from them." The scroll never creates a task, so it points back to the [Goals board](goals.md).
- **Linked entry vs its canonical task:** a live linked entry shows the task's **current** content (a rename on the Goals board shows through) and its **current** checked state (a completion or reopen there is reflected; a stale local mark never wins). Once the task is deleted the entry renders from its stored snapshot text as a plain entry.
- **Mantra step:** shows its line inline (fixed or the day's pool line), no reader; checked by the circle. Sealed or not, the words stay visible.
- **Rituals rail, reset countdown:** always shown under the subtitle, ticking each minute toward the next 4am; "under a minute" in the final stretch.
- **Roadmap, evening:** the builder always shows the count set for tomorrow; entries reorder/remove inline.
- **Note, evening:** the ✳ field mirrors the saved note (another device's edit lands unless mid-edit); after the seal it is read-only ("Left for the morning") and hidden entirely if empty.
- **Note, morning:** present → the gold ✳ card leads the scroll, read-only; absent → nothing renders (no empty-state nag).
- **Reader open:** page scroll locked, close always visible; finish confirmation then a pinned red Done button — release is explicit, never automatic (ADR 0013).
- **Rail with no practices:** a quiet invitation + the single add.
- **Deleting from the rail:** always behind the warning box; the confirm names the real consequence (gone from every day).

## 6. Edge cases

- **Past midnight (4am rollover), item added after checks, delete-all honored, double confirm, unchecking after seal refused, clock skew, two devices, signed out:** all as v1 / ADR 0009 — unchanged by the restructure.
- **Roadmap set at 1:30am** targets the same morning as one set at 23:00 (ADR 0012; tested on the boundary, month, and year lines). The note rides the same key, so the same holds for it.
- **Note emptied after being written:** `morningNote.set` with blank text deletes the row — the morning never opens on a blank note.
- **Roadmap entries done ≠ ritual sealed:** entries carry their own `doneAt`; the component's check is auto-set when the last entry is walked but stays manually checkable (an empty-roadmap morning can be checked through). A **linked** entry's displayed done state follows the canonical `goalTasks` row, not its local `doneAt`.
- **Picking the same task twice / a stale picker:** `addFromTask` is a no-op (returns null) if that task is already on the day, so a double-tap or a not-yet-refreshed picker never creates a duplicate. A checked or "waiting" task is rejected at the mutation, not just hidden from `availableTasks`.
- **Linked task renamed / completed / reopened elsewhere:** `forDay` resolves the task's current content and checked state each read, so a Goals-board rename, completion, or reopen shows through the next render with no roadmap edit.
- **Linked task deleted:** the entry falls back to its stored snapshot `text`, drops its `goalTaskId` from the resolved row (renders as a plain legacy entry), and from then on tracks only its own `doneAt` (nothing canonical left to mirror). The Todoist push only fires while the link is live.
- **Legacy free-form roadmap entries** (written before ARI-144, or via `roadmap.add`): keep rendering and behaving exactly as before: their own `text`, `note`, and `doneAt`, no canonical mirror. Documented as preserved; the scroll simply no longer offers a free-form add.
- **Blueprint read with no document yet:** opening the reader adopts the document first (idempotent); the step's preview says the words live in the Blueprint.
- **A question step with no fixed words** rotates deterministically per ritual day — the same question all day on every device, a new one after 4am.
- **Cutoff crossed with the page open:** the reachable beat is computed once per mount (like the day keys), so a tab left open across 17:00 or 4:00 still shows the old beat until the next visit/refresh. Accepted, consistent with the day-key handling.
- **An unfinished beat at its cutoff just passes:** if the morning was never sealed by 17:00 it simply becomes unreachable — no nag, no penalty; its partial state persists in the day's row as history, and every submission made in it stays in the Today log.
- **Deleting the roadmap/question components** is honored like any delete (one-shot upgrade never returns them); the "+ roadmap" edit button hides while one exists.
- **Legacy "read the mantra" items** (v1 `read` kind with inline `content`, behind the Read button) are **folded into the inline `mantra` kind by the v4 reconcile** — their words are kept, but they now show inline (not behind the Read button), and any duplicate seeded mantra beside them is removed so there is exactly one. This is the single deliberate exception to ADR 0011's additive-only rule, scoped to the mantra duplicates and the one stale default question string.
- **Morning question with fixed words** (a person typed their own prompt) overrides the settings-driven journal and hides the prompt picker — it is theirs, not the Daily Exercise's. Emptying its words returns it to the journal.

## 7. AI involvement

None at runtime. The evening question bank (`lib/questions.ts`) and the mantra pool (`lib/mantras.ts`) are code-resident with deterministic per-ritual-day rotation; the morning journal prompt is chosen by the person's Daily Exercise setting, not generated (deliberately: no AI generation in this pass). The VoiceField's transcription/shaping applies to question answers as everywhere else. Parked: the Coach proposing components, drift signals off completion history, adaptive question/mantra selection, Core-pulled daily tidbits + an inspirational image/quote in the scroll, and the always-on ambient mantra surface (see the [Coach KB mantra pool](../coach-knowledge-base/mantras.md)).

## 8. Data touched

Exact shapes in [`../../architecture/data-model.md`](../../architecture/data-model.md).

**Owned:**
- `ritualItems`: `{ userId, ritual: morning|night|any, kind: do|read|mantra|question|roadmap|tidbit, title, content?, source?, order, createdAt, updatedAt }` — the typed components (ADR 0011). `content` = a fixed mantra line / inline read words / a fixed question / a pinned tidbit line (empty mantras and questions rotate their pool/bank; an empty `tidbit` is filled by the [daily-quote agent](daily-tidbit.md), whose text lives in `dailyTidbits`, not here); `source` (reads) = `inline` | `blueprint`; `ritual: "any"` is a time-indifferent practice (kind `do` only, enforced server-side).
- `ritualDays`: per-day check state + completion history, kind-blind (ADR 0009). Rows exist per assignment (`morning`, `night`, `any`); `any` rows carry daily checks but are never sealed (`rituals.complete` only accepts morning/night).
- `roadmapEntries`: `{ userId, day, text, note?, goalTaskId?, order, doneAt?, createdAt }`, indexed `by_user_day [userId, day, order]` — the roadmap loop's rows, keyed by TARGET day (ADR 0012). `goalTaskId?` links the entry to a canonical `goalTasks` row (ARI-144); when set, `text` is only a delete-time snapshot and the live task's content / checked state win at read time. Legacy / free-form entries leave it unset.
- `morningNotes`: `{ userId, day, text, createdAt, updatedAt }`, indexed `by_user_day [userId, day]` — the note to morning-you, one per TARGET morning (same key convention as the roadmap); upsert on write, deleted when emptied.

**Writes elsewhere:** `settings.ritualsSeededAt` / `settings.ritualsSeedVersion` (seed + upgrade markers); `settings.dailyExercise` (the morning journal's inline prompt picker); `interactions` (`ritual_completed`, `ritual_question`); `blueprint` (only via the idempotent adopt); `goalTasks` (a linked entry's morning check mirrors `checked` / `completedAt` back to the canonical task, ARI-144); Todoist (`internal.todoist.pushChecked`, when the linked task carries a `todoistTaskId`).

**Drawn:** `blueprint.content` (blueprint-sourced reads), `settings.dailyExercise` (the morning journal prompt), `goalTasks` + `goals` (the pickable open tasks and their goal names; a linked entry's live content and checked state), the clock.

**Code:** `convex/rituals.ts` (typed components, the seed, the v4 upgrade + `reconcileMorningV4`), `convex/roadmap.ts` (the loop store + the ARI-144 link: `availableTasks`, `addFromTask`, canonical resolution in `forDay`, the mirror-through in `setDone`), `convex/morningNote.ts`, `convex/blueprintDoc.ts`, `convex/settings.ts` (`dailyExercise`), `lib/ritual.ts` (cutoffs, day keys, spans, `nextRitualDayKey`, `msUntilRollover` + `formatCountdown` for the rail's reset timer, `scrollProgress` for the header's spine-only count), `lib/questions.ts` (the evening bank + `journalPromptFor` for the settings-driven morning journal), `lib/mantras.ts` (the pool), `components/today/RitualSequence.tsx` (sequence + edit mode + inline mantra editor + inline daily-exercise picker + Goals-task roadmap picker + locked tabs + separate `<RitualSeal>`), `components/today/RitualsRail.tsx` (daily practice rail + reset timer), `components/today/KeepingUp.tsx` (last-14-day strip + gentle run + started/finished/empty pip states), `components/today/VoiceField.tsx`, `components/today/DailyRead.tsx`, `components/today/ImmersiveReader.tsx`, `components/today/MorningNote.tsx`, `app/page.tsx` (composition order: ritual → horizons → seal).
