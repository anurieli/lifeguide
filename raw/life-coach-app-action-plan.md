# Life Coach App: Build Plan and Path to Partnering

## The honest read on where you are

You have a strong product instinct (build for yourself first, find what is universal) and a clear thesis (an integrated, context-aware coach beats fragmented tools). What you do not have yet is a spine. You have been building wide before locking the core, which is why it feels like drowning. Nothing here is wasted. You just need to sequence it.

Your real advantage: you ARE the target user, you run an AI integration company, and you already think in agents and context orchestration. The technical risk is lower than you feel. The actual risk is focus and validation, not whether you can build it.

---

## Step 1: Lock the core bet before anything else

From the market scan, nobody has nailed one specific thing: a coach that reads across ALL your context (brain dumps, metadata, sources, the changelog) and a home base you actually return to every morning and night. Vision boards exist. AI journals exist. Coaching chatbots exist. The integrated, context-aware loop does not.

So the bet to prove is this:

> Does a context-aware coach plus a daily anchor actually change my behavior more than the scattered tools I already use?

If that is true for you, you have something real. If it is not, no amount of features fixes it. Everything below serves answering that question.

---

## Step 2: Cut to the one loop (your real MVP)

Stop building features. Build one loop, end to end, for yourself only:

1. **Capture**: brain dump a thought, with metadata attached (where you are, what you were doing, the source like a podcast or song).
2. **Context**: the system stores it in a structure the coach can read across entries.
3. **Anchor**: a morning and night home screen that surfaces what matters, what to remember, what you are falling behind on.
4. **Track**: the changelog logs the deltas over time so the coach sees progress versus drift.

That is the spine. Vision board, to-do matching, health data, location, proactive nudges: all of it comes later. If the spine does not make YOU show up daily, the rest is decoration.

---

## Step 3: Who to talk to (and what to ask)

You named the gap yourself: you have never coached anyone. Close that gap by talking to four groups. You are not pitching. You are learning.

### 1. Life coaches (3 to 5 of them) — domain experts
The point: understand the actual methodology so your coach is built on something real, not vibes.
- How do you actually structure a coaching relationship over time?
- Where do clients fall off, and what brings them back?
- What does the first session look like versus the tenth?
- What signals tell you someone is stuck versus growing?
- If you could see everything a client was thinking between sessions, what would you do with it?

### 2. Solo or small-team consumer AI founders (2 to 3) — builders who shipped
Target people behind apps like Rosebud or Mindsera, or anyone who built a daily-habit consumer app.
- What actually drives retention in a daily app? What did you wrongly think would?
- How did you handle context and memory at scale without it getting messy or expensive?
- What would you cut if you started over?
- Solo or partner, and why?

### 3. Potential users (5 to 10) — people who match the profile
Ambitious, mentally scattered, too much on their plate, struggle to stay on top of goals.
- Walk me through how you currently keep track of your goals and thoughts. (Watch what they actually do, not what they say they should do.)
- What have you tried and dropped? Why did you drop it?
- When in your day do thoughts pile up with nowhere to go?

### 4. A potential technical or product partner — but later, see Step 5
Only after the spine is scoped. You cannot recruit a strong partner to a blob.

---

## Step 4: Validate cheaply before you build more

Two things in parallel over the next two weeks:

- **Use the closest competitors daily.** Mindsera (visualization plus AI insight, closest to your vision) and Rosebud (daily journaling habit anchor). Living in them for two weeks will teach you more about what is missing than any analysis. Note every moment you wish it did something it did not.
- **Write the one-loop spec** (Step 2) as a tight document. Not a feature list. The loop, the data model for context and metadata, and the morning/night anchor behavior. This becomes the thing you build OR the thing you hand a partner.

---

## Step 5: The build-versus-partner decision

Do not decide this yet. Decide it after Steps 1 to 4. Here is the framing so you can decide cleanly when you get there.

**Build a thin slice yourself first if**: you want to keep full control of the vision, the spine is small enough to prototype solo, and you want to validate on yourself before spending equity or money. Given your skill set, this is very doable and probably the right first move.

**Bring in a partner when**: you have a validated spine and you need something you genuinely lack. Be specific about what that is:
- Design and product polish (consumer apps live or die on feel)
- Coaching domain depth (a coach co-founder closes your methodology gap)
- Distribution (someone who can get it in front of the right people)
- Sustained velocity you cannot maintain solo while running CTE

A partner is not a way to escape the lost feeling. It multiplies clarity you already have. So get the clarity first, then multiply it.

**What to ask a potential builder or partner:**
- What do you think the riskiest assumption is here? (Tests whether they think or just execute.)
- If you had to ship one loop in 30 days, what would you cut?
- Have you shipped a consumer product where people came back daily? What made them?
- How would you handle the context and memory architecture?

---

## Your next two weeks, concretely

1. Write the one-loop spec (the spine, the context model, the anchor).
2. Use Mindsera and Rosebud every single day. Keep a running "I wish it did X" list.
3. Talk to 3 life coaches and 5 people who match the user profile.
4. Decide: prototype the spine solo, or scope it for a partner.

After that, you will not be aimless. You will have a validated core, real domain input, and a clear build path. Then the roadmap writes itself.
