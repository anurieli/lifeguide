# Sources · Self-orientation, ritual, identity, and behavior change

**Status:** raw source map · **Opened:** 2026-06-06

Use this as a reading list for LifeGuide's self-map and coaching model. The product translation is intentionally brief so the source can be revisited without locking the design too early.

---

## Identity and being lost

### Emerging adulthood

- Jeffrey Jensen Arnett, "Emerging Adulthood: A Theory of Development From the Late Teens Through the Twenties"
- Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/12476725_Emerging_Adulthood_A_Theory_of_Development_From_the_Late_Teens_Through_the_Twenties
- Useful because: frames feeling lost, unstable, in-between, and exploratory as a developmental phase rather than a personal defect.
- Product implication: LifeGuide should normalize exploration while helping the person convert exploration into commitments.

### Self-authorship

- Marcia Baxter Magolda, "Helping Students Make Their Way to Adulthood: Good Company for the Journey"
- Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/108648220200600602
- Useful because: focuses on moving from externally defined choices toward internally authored identity, values, and decisions.
- Product implication: the app should help a person stop outsourcing identity and start authoring their own standards.

## Future self and vision

### Possible selves

- Hazel Markus and Paula Nurius, "Possible Selves"
- Link: https://web.stanford.edu/~hazelm/publications/1986_Markus%20%26%20Nurius_PossibleSelves.pdf
- Useful because: hoped-for selves and feared selves can guide motivation, behavior, and identity change.
- Product implication: the Future Self should include both what the person wants to become and what they refuse to become.

### Self-discrepancy / standards

- "Self-Standards and Self-Discrepancies. A Structural Model of Self-Knowledge"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4023076/
- Useful because: helps model gaps between actual self, ideal self, and standards.
- Product implication: LifeGuide can compute alignment gaps without shaming the user.

## Motivation and values

### Self-Determination Theory

- Richard Ryan and Edward Deci, "Self-Determination Theory and the Facilitation of Intrinsic Motivation, Social Development, and Well-Being"
- Link: https://www.selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2000_RyanDeci_SDT.pdf
- Useful because: durable motivation depends heavily on autonomy, competence, and relatedness.
- Product implication: the Coach should support agency, skill-building, and connection instead of defaulting to pressure.

### Acceptance and Commitment Therapy values

- "Assessing the valuing process in Acceptance and Commitment Therapy"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6905635/
- Useful because: ACT connects values, psychological flexibility, discomfort, and committed action.
- Product implication: values should not stay abstract; they should connect to tolerated discomfort and concrete action.

### ACT for health behavior change

- "Acceptance and Commitment Therapy for Health Behavior Change: A Contextually-Driven Approach"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5769281/
- Useful because: translates ACT into behavior change contexts.
- Product implication: a coaching lens can ask, "What value is this serving, and what action would express it today?"

## Behavior change and habits

### COM-B / Behaviour Change Wheel

- Susan Michie, Maartje van Stralen, and Robert West, "The behaviour change wheel: A new method for characterising and designing behaviour change interventions"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3096582/
- Useful because: behavior depends on capability, opportunity, and motivation.
- Product implication: bad habit advice should diagnose the actual blocker instead of saying "be disciplined."

### Habit formation

- Phillippa Lally, Cornelia van Jaarsveld, Henry Potts, and Jane Wardle, "How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world"
- Link: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ejsp.674
- Useful because: habits form through context-dependent repetition, and the timeline varies widely.
- Product implication: ritual consistency matters, but missed days should not collapse the system.

### Motivational Interviewing

- Brian Burke, Hal Arkowitz, and Marisa Menchola, "The efficacy of motivational interviewing: a meta-analysis of controlled clinical trials"
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14516234/
- Useful because: helps people change when they are ambivalent.
- Product implication: when the user resists change, the Coach should surface ambivalence and elicit reasons, not lecture.

### Motivational Interviewing theory

- "Toward a Theory of Motivational Interviewing"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2759607/
- Useful because: explains mechanisms and clinical use cases behind MI.
- Product implication: a Coach lens can use reflective listening, change talk, readiness, and confidence.

## Stabilization and consistency

### Behavioral activation

- "Behavioural activation for depression; an update of meta-analysis of effectiveness and sub group analysis"
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24936656/
- Useful because: mood often follows meaningful action; small scheduled behaviors can stabilize a stuck person.
- Product implication: LifeGuide should generate low-friction stabilizing actions, especially when someone is drifting or numb.

### Behavioral activation, digital interventions

- "Internet-based behavioral activation for depression: a systematic review"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10251223/
- Useful because: relevant to digital rituals and guided self-help.
- Product implication: the ritual stream can include tiny behavior commitments, not just reflection.

## Relationships, social environment, and influence

### Social relationships and mortality

- Julianne Holt-Lunstad, Timothy Smith, and J. Bradley Layton, "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk: A Meta-analytic Review"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2910600/
- Useful because: social relationships are not a soft side issue; they are central to health and stability.
- Product implication: relationships and social environment should be first-class parts of the self-map.

### Men's help-seeking

- "Improving Mental Health Service Utilization Among Men: A Systematic Review and Synthesis of Behavior Change Techniques Within Interventions Targeting Help-Seeking"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6560805/
- Useful because: gives behavior-change techniques for improving engagement among men.
- Product implication: for male users, frame support as agency, capacity, and self-command rather than weakness.

### Male mental health campaigns

- "The impact of media-based mental health campaigns on male help-seeking: a systematic review"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11369358/
- Useful because: reviews how mental health messages land with men.
- Product implication: tone matters. The app should avoid infantilizing or generic therapeutic language.

## Meaning and purpose

### Meaning in life interventions

- "A Meta-Analytic Review of Interventions That Promote Meaning in Life"
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33626890/
- Useful because: meaning and purpose can be influenced through intervention, not only discovered passively.
- Product implication: LifeGuide can actively help users construct purpose through reflection, commitments, and evidence.

### Meaning-centered interventions

- "Effects of Psychosocial Interventions on Meaning and Purpose in Adults with Cancer"
- Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6602826/
- Useful because: shows how meaning-centered work is operationalized in serious contexts.
- Product implication: purpose prompts should be grounded and serious, not inspirational fluff.
