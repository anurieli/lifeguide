## 1. Crafting Your Persona

If you're here, it's because you have a vision for who you want to become and you want help getting there. So the first step is to write out that vision as clear as day. This section is about defining who you are, and who you want to become, in writing. Let’s get your thoughts out of your head, and shine a spotlight on them so you can **SEE** the ideal persona you’ve been thinking about for so long.


### 🟢 Note to Self  ·  `green`

You are here for a reason, why? What do you want to tell yourself? Who do you want to be? When you think of the brother, sister, mother, father, friend, co-worker, boss, *HUMAN* you want to be...... what does that person look like. Be specific, be open. This is the first thing you are seeing because this "exercise" opens up our minds to what's to follow - it's a method of looking inward.


**Example:**

eg. "I want to be the person that everyone knows for doing ... I am humble but assertive...and I will..."


### 🟡 Role to Embody  ·  `yellow`

Give a persona definition to the person I described above. Get creative, as this persona is for me and only me.


_Basically just a simple "shortcut" way to remembering everything you want to become (note to yourself) by attaching it to a Persona. Kind of like creating a character that has all the attributes you aspire for._


**Example:**

ex. The Respected Military Commander: Someone who people trust to lead them in and out of war. Instills a sense of calmness in ALL of his people, and loves them all like his sons. Someone who thinks "how yes" instead of "why not" to do something.


### 🟡 Values to Live By  ·  `yellow`

List of values that I want to embody - and why. Include both those I think I already attain, as well as those I want to acquire.


**Example:**

- Accountable (Responsible): Someone that people trust to get the job done
- Disciplined: the superpower of doing what I want when I want to do it.
- Kind: Make even my enemy into my friend.
- Patient: Good things come to those who wait. And rushing never produced anything of value.


### 🟢 Role Models  ·  `green`

Figures I look up to and why. Can be characters from shows, athlete, people from your life, ANYONE that you want to take something from.


_These are like giving shorter names to the values you want to live by (mentioned above). Basically people that you want to remember (instead of remembering ideals and values because those are boding and hard to remember). but the people we like and admire are easy to remember... so im just helping you write them down on paper... you already think of them._


**Example:**

- Richard Branson - Do the unthinkable, whilst attracting everyone to you.
- Harvey Spector - Looks the part; doesn't bite his nails in a meeting, people take him seriously
- Michael Jordan - Doesn't give excuses, but brings results. People trust him to get the job done when it matters
- Friend 1 (professionalism and example)
- Friend 2 (Composure and interpersonal skills)
- Friend 3 - Started from nothing, I watched him. Showed the power of consistency and passion.
- The future wife/husband - Someone worth improving for


### 🟢 Weaknesses  ·  `green`

The things that are most detrimental to your progress. Things that you have been battling for ages, and have tried time and time again to rid yourself of them. Weaknesses are distractions that directly influence your ability to achieve your goals: whether daily to-dos or year-long missions. Be brutal and honest with yourself here... this is for you.


_In order to move forward, we must be extremely aware of what's holding us back so that we don't waste energy. Sometimes, the sheer fact that we become actively aware of our downfalls, and call them what they are, they stop getting in the way. They are called weaknesses and not merely distractions because these are the things that consistently throw you off your game._


**Example:**

- Sleeping in - I just always end my day thinking I could have done more, and there's so much I want to do that I could If I just wake up earlier.
- Weed/beers (any vice really) -
- ADHD (jump to the next thing) -


### 🔴 My Mantra  ·  `red`

Your personal mission statement to keep you focused.


_This would be ideal to put everywhere: lock screen, on your case, and anywhere else your eyes gravitate towards. (Tattoo maybe 👀)_


**Example:**

eg. "I will create something that people can't stop talking about, that even I won't believe I did."


### 🟢 Strengths  ·  `green`

What are you good at? What do you feel confident doing? What do you *excel* at?

Let yourself go wild here, don't hold back. Even if no one would agree with you, write it down. Maybe people dont see it because you aren't expressing it enough! *That's why you are here...*


_It’s crucial to identify our strengths because we often overlook the areas where we truly shine. If we don’t take the time to reflect on and appreciate what we’re good at, we might miss out on understanding our own potential. Recognizing our strengths helps us build confidence and gives us a foundation to tackle life’s challenges. It’s like picking the low-hanging fruit—starting with what comes naturally allows us to gain momentum and skills, preparing us to take on bigger, more demanding goals over time._


**Malleability:** This should always be refined, and added to accordingly. The longer the lest the better!


**Example:**

- Working with people
- leading teams
- assertive when needed
- very direct, does not hold back what needs to be said

## 2. Setting Your Goals

After crafting our persona, in as much as we could get it in writing, let's turn up the heat and start defining goals. This is where most progression dies – *inability to define a concrete goals*. 

The reason is that in an age when we can have anything and everything all at once, we find it difficult to settle on one thing to go after. The problem with that is we end up aimless wandering through life, wasting our precious time and resources getting nowhere, wondering why we aren’t living the life we want. Effectively, by not setting real goals you want to work towards in life, you are letting life decide for you. 

So accept that if you want to successfully move forward, you must decide where you are going. You must set concrete goals. We will make this process as easy as can be. You wrote a note to yourself, you know what kind of person you want to become. Now lets leverage that and start from our high level goals (less concrete), and slowly work our way down to our daily (ultra concrete) goals.


## ❓ **WTF is a Concrete Goal?**

  - **Measurability** - It’s finite, and there is a defined trigger that will check it off your list.

    - *Climbing Everest – do I want to reach its peak? Just hit base camp? climb 2ft up and call it a day?*

  -  **Time Horizon** (Deadline): When do you want to accomplish it by?


### 🟡 Life Goals  ·  `yellow`

Write down at least 5 BIG life goals. Then challenge yourself to write down a reason for that goal (if it's just a dream goal without a reason, that's fine!). Go crazy, don't sell myself short. This is for my eyes only.

 * *No need for a finite time-horizon here - but advised!*


_The point of this is to put all your dreams in front of your eyes to show you just how much you want to accomplish. This makes smaller goals seem more manageable while also lighting a fire beneath your ass to get you going. If its seems like a lot more than expected, good. The pressure is on. You may be surprised at what you learn about your goals. Reconsider them at your discretion._


**Example:**

- *Climb Everest:* - childhood dream, and is the mental / physical challenge that will turn me into the person I aspire to become
- *Start my dream [clothing] company:* I have all these designs and one of clothes that I made, that my friends love, and I aspire to work for myself so that I have the freedom to do as I choose and be independent… and work towards my goal as opposed to someone else. 
- *Backpack Across Asia:* I want to experience the freedom that comes with backpacking, and I feel like the only way to learn about the culture (and myself) is to do this thing. 
- *Own a house by age 30:* (in another county if you’re bold) -  I see it as a manifestation of stability and commitment*


### 🟡 5 Year Goal  ·  `yellow`

How do I want your life to look like in 5 Years? Get SPECIFIC, and don't be afraid. Considering where you are at now, and trusting that life works in your favor when you tell it where you want to go.


_Basically keeping your eye on some ball, and priming your subconscious to manifest it in your daily actions._


**Malleability:** This is dynamic, but try not to change it too much. Little pivots are fine, but unless your whole world turns upside down, dont change it more than 15%.


**Example:**

- I will be living in Israel and making money from the US
- I will be self-employed, with the work revolving around bettering individuals...
- I will be valued at 5 million+ USD
 - *I will consistently earn enough that I can support a wife and child, travel wherever whenever and however, able to buy the best if my heart desires.* 
- *I will be best friends with my sisters
*- *I WILL have a safe house (land and a humble abode)* 
 - *I will be leading a community*


### 🔴 Yearly Goals  ·  `red`

Build on your 5 year goal. Shouldn't be more than 3, preferably 2 or less. The less, the better as you will be putting 100% of your energy into getting it done. For every goal, describe a little about what you need to do to get there. Don't forget to set a finishing date!

*If more than 1, write them down in order of priority.*


**Malleability:** Try and go at least 4 months going after this goal before rushing to change. Again, if you want to pivot your strategy thats fine, but you can just pivot whenever you want, or when it gets hard, or when you have had enough. Get over the hump by just sticking to a goal you set. Prove to yourself just once that you can actually get done what you wrote down on paper.


**Example:**

- Goal 1 - [DEADLINE]
- how you plan to get it done?
- Goal 2 - [DEADLINE]
- how you plan to get it done?


### 🔴 Monthly Goals  ·  `red`

You can do a lot in a month. Decide on a habit you want to start, or break. Show yourself you are capable of achieving the goals you set in the short-term, and the long-term won't be so daunting.  Get good at hitting your monthly goals, and you’ll be on track to hitting your yearly goals.


**Malleability:** Change once a month. Every repetition you should learn more about your capacity to set and achieve monthly goals, and this should influence your future monthly goals. If you realize you set unrealistic goals, change them, but you need to find it in you to set at least 1 monthly goal that just doesnt change till yo accomplish it.


**Example:**

- Keep up 5 am routine 1

    - Get 8 hours of documented work a day (Using a tracker!!!!)

    - Learn to work in shorter chunks (less than 2 hours)

    -- Basically learn to get in the zone faster …

    -- Practice this once a day. When I dont want to work, but I know I have no excuse not to…. JUSTst get on and try and get into the zone

    - Practice Saying “Hello” to people 1st

    - READ THIS LIST EVERY DAY FOR A MONTH


### 🟡 Daily Goals  ·  `yellow`

Things that can compound and have a profound impact on my life. Small things, that can easily compound. Get good at hitting your daily goals, and you'll be on track to hitting your yearly goals.


_(Max 30 min TOTAL, 3 tasks is more than enough for starters.)_


**Example:**

- EX. Learn Arabic 10 min a day: useful skill for the life I want to live
- Read Something, anything, for 10 min a day: learn something new every day
- Meditate 10 min a day: callous the mind to make everything else in my day-to-day easier.


### 🔴 Paint a North Star  ·  `red`

After jotting down all the small details, now you are ready to paint a vivid image of your ultimate aspiration—the guiding beacon that directs all your smaller goals from daily tasks to long-term objectives...


_By articulating this overarching goal, you create a motivational compass that helps navigate life's decisions, ensuring they align with your fundamental aspirations. Reflect deeply and describe this vision in a few sentences, making it both vivid and precise..._


**Malleability:** Just remember the the idea behind the North Star (yes the one in the sky) is that it just doesnt change. It always points to the same direction. Use everything we built unitl this point to decide one 1 place to which you are heading and stick to it.


**Example:**

eg. While Im still 26, nearing 27, 4 years, age 30, I will be self employed, have over a million to my name, and I will throw a celebratory birthday weekend with the people that are closest to me and helped me get there. I will fly mom and dad out to wherever is needed. On that day, they will both know that they dont need to worry anymore. Oh, and I will have a big piece of land, somewhere hard to get to, with a small plane that can take me anywhere and can house my family and their family. I will have a house [here] and [there], traveling with my family twice a year. I will have weekly hangouts with my friends, and monthly “im alive” parties… 
- eg. By the time I turn 30, I will have transitioned from law school to becoming an influential civil rights attorney. I will have established myself in a non-profit organization that focuses on fighting for equal rights and justice. By then I will have argued at least one case in front of a federal court, contributing to significant legal reforms. I envision using my legal skills to empower underrepresented communities, and to have published articles that educate the public on their rights and the importance of civic engagement. My ultimate aim is to be a voice for change, ensuring that justice is accessible for all
- eg. In the next five years, I envision myself having successfully shifted from my current job in retail management to a fulfilling career in environmental science. This change will align with my passion for conservation and my commitment to making a positive impact on the planet. I plan to be involved in major environmental projects, possibly even leading initiatives that focus on sustainable practices within local communities. By the age of 35, I see myself as a key contributor to influential environmental policies, working closely with both governmental and non-governmental organizations. Alongside my career goals, I aim to balance my professional life with a nurturing family environment, supporting my spouse in their career aspirations and together, building a home that thrives on mutual respect, love, and shared values toward a greener world. Oh, and I will  have 2 children by then.

## 3. Forging Your Mindset

This section is about developing the mental resilience and positive mindset necessary to overcome challenges and stay committed to your goals. We will make it clear how your life is going to change, and implement proven methods to getting these things to stick.


### 🟡 Expectations  ·  `yellow`

You now know where you're going, and you know that the life you have painted for yourself is different than your current one. This means that change is inevitable, and that the things you were used to may need to be changed. So write down all the ways which you believe your new life will affect your old life. What tough situations do you expect to be in now that you are trying to incorporate new things into your life (while also removing old ones.


_What routines will need to change, what tough decisions will you need to make. Even if you dont execute right away, even before you do you need to set your expectations about your life will change. in the begging git will be uncomfortable, as you are not used to it, but before you know it you will have shed the old skin that was holding you back. Oh, and dont forget to describe your reason for each. 
Go as deep as you can into each expectation. The catch here is “expect”. When you are faced with a hard decision, you brain will automatically say “Wait, I expected this when I said I was going to change my life” and it wont crumble in the face of temptation because it has a reason for what its doing._


**Example:**

- *I’ll have to start waking up early - so I can start working on my side hustle BEFORE going to work*

    - *I’ll need to distance myself from [X] as they [are a bad influence], holding me back, etc..*

    - *Need to stop smoking - I want to run a marathon, and this isn’t good for training.*

    - *I wont be able to eat the same food I’m used to [chips, candy, etc] because they will work against my weight goal*

    - *No longer will I be able to hangout with the squad every night, ill have to start saying no - its leading me to sleep late and I’ll most likely smoke weed.*


### 🟢 Some WHY's  ·  `green`

Dynamic list of things I do in my life, and the inherent reason I am doing them. Make sure to include a short reason for doing this. If you know why, you will know how. *You can include things that are in your daily and monthly goals.


_This is a collection of things that are a part of your life, or will be, and you are now giving them a solid reason as to why they need to exist..._


**Malleability:** These are meant to change with you!


**Example:**

- Read Philosophy: to expand English abilities, expand my ability to think, Learn new ideas

- Finishing my degree: to start what I finish, and to have a door-opening tool in the kit

- Stop Smoking: it really is just a minor set back for the days to come. Lazier, hazier, less motivated… all for 3 hours of “fun”. **It takes the edge off, but I need the edge.**


### 🟢 Time Tracking  ·  `green`

How do you spend most of your time? Map your last week, your last day, the last few hours? What are you doing on the day to day, hour to hour? We want you to be aware

- *If you know where you err, you will know how to correct.*


_It is a great habit to track you time. With the speed of life these days we get to the end of the week feeling like we did so much, but for some reason our results prove otherwise.
In the army we had nightly meetings where all the officers shared what we planned for that day. We then compared it to what we actually got done, and finally a near minute-by-minute analysis of what we did that day that led to our success, or failure.
It took me a while, but when I figured that if I can just reflect on what I do with my time, I could actually take control of it.

*The first step is awareness!*_


**Malleability:** Ideally, every day! Obviously I dont expect you to come back every day, but take this field as the most dynamic field of them all because every day is a new


**Example:**

- over the last week I was building Lifeguide. I spend maybe 8 hours a day on it.

- I also had long lunches/dinners (longer than I want but Ill work on it)

- i had a coffe chat with a friend today that turned into a 3 hours social session. I didnt even think about it till I wrote it down here.


### 🟢 Flash Reminders  ·  `green`

Just some quick things you want to remind yourself of every day. Can be as long as you want, just keep in mind, the longer it is, the more annoying it will be to read every day.


_These are some of mine, feel free to take them!_


**Malleability:** These are meant to change with you!


**Example:**

- Every decision can be the best decision, as long as it is looked at as such
- Turn every situation into a positive one
- When things truly take a turn for the worse, remember that all you can do is work to turn everything back to positive state.
- As long as you're not dying, everything will be ok
- Be the person the world will look up to (so everyone matters)


### 🟡 A Quote  ·  `yellow`

Put here ONE quote that you like and is fitting for the overall mindset you are going for. This can always change, but pick one.


**Malleability:** Change it to whatever quote made you feel some type of way today! This is for you.


**Example:**

Imagine you're talking to god about the person you want to become...
- You say "I want to be courageous."
+ God replies "Then I will give you monsters to terrify you. That way you can conquer them."
- You say "I want to be patient."
+ God replies "Then I will make you work harder and longer. That way you can learn to wait."
- You say "I want to be wise."
+ God replies "Then I will give you failures that will crush your spirit. That way you can learn the value of judgment."
- You then say... "That sounds like a hard life. Can you give me a good life?"
+ God replies "Just like we measure the quality of a blacksmith by the strength of his steel, I measure you by what you are at the end, not the fire and the hammer it took to make you. A good life isn't an easy life. A good life makes you into a good person. And that, my child, is a hard life."
