# The Blueprint for Living: an 8-pillar daily-conduct doctrine

> Provenance: synthesized 2026-07-12 from seven Instagram reels Ariel saved to a bookmark folder named "Blueprint". Canonical research note lives in Brain Vault at `01-Projects/lifeguide/lifeguide-notes/concepts/the-blueprint-for-living.md`. This copy exists because the doctrine seeds the **Daily Ritual** feature.

## What this document is

A doctrine document: a prescriptive standard for how to conduct an ordinary day. Not a vision (where you are going), not a goal set (what you will achieve by when), not a review (what happened). It answers one question: how do I carry myself through a normal day, on purpose. In coaching terms it is the standards sheet the Coach holds a person against.

Its realm is **daily conduct** (self-governance, the practiced day), sitting between identity and outcomes:

```
Identity (who I am, values)        <- self-knowledge realm
   |
Daily conduct (how I run a day)    <- THIS document's realm
   |
Outcomes (goals hit, life built)   <- goal-setting realm
```

## Role in the product

1. **Doctrine layer**: content the Coach draws on when a user asks "what should my days look like?" A starter rules-of-life template a user can adopt and edit.
2. **Diagnostic checklist**: eight pillars = eight axes to score a user's current life against.
3. **Drift detector**: signals a user has slipped out of conduct (consuming more than creating, no exciting goals, comfort creep).
4. **Payoff vocabulary**: every practice carries a "why it pays off" line; the Coach sells practices in benefit language, not command language.
5. **Daily Ritual seed**: the morning/night ritual defaults derive from this doctrine.

## Values it encodes

Discipline over motivation. Environment over willpower. Creation over consumption. Depth over scatter. Presence over performance. Self-trust through kept promises. Small actions compounding. Deliberate inputs (people, media, food).

## The framework: pillar -> practice -> payoff

### 1. The Body
Train for strength and capacity 5 to 6 times a week; walk daily; whole foods, protein first, stop at 80 percent full (hara hachi bu); sleep 7 to 9 hours; schedule recovery before burnout forces it; cut alcohol. Payoff: energy, stress tolerance, confidence, the engine that funds every other pillar.

### 2. The Inner Game
Get clear on the why behind the goals (ikigai); work on yourself before chasing a partner; treat mistakes as golden repair (kintsugi); start before ready (wabi-sabi); break comfort on purpose; borrow belief from someone who already did it. Payoff: discipline stops feeling like force and starts feeling like direction.

### 3. Systems and Discipline
One small win a day (kaizen); consistency over motivation (keizoku); keep promises to yourself; protect mornings; automate repeat decisions (meals, workout times, wardrobe); shape the environment (seiri and seiton); split the day into selfless hours (family, clients, team) and selfish hours (training, thinking, learning); say no to the unaligned. Payoff: willpower spent only where it matters; the day stops being reactive.

### 4. Focus and Creation
A pre-work focus ritual (shuchu); depth on few things over scatter on many; create more than you consume; one day a week off social media. Payoff: consumption drains hunger, creation feeds it.

### 5. Direction
One huge goal that scares you, broken down to today; plan tomorrow before bed; weekly audit (what gets reviewed gets improved); journal wins and lessons; track progress not perfection; never compare timelines; become the person your younger self would look up to. Payoff: drift only survives when nobody is looking at it.

### 6. Money and Craft
Invest now, the habit not the amount; track every dollar; invest in yourself before consuming; learn one skill that makes money; start the business, post before ready, build the brand. Payoff: freedom, options, time back later in life.

### 7. People
Average of the five around you, so get proximity to people ahead of you; cut energy drains; real time with family, calls over texts; be a good human, feedback over criticism. Payoff: ceilings and energy are contagious in both directions.

### 8. Presence
Slow steady movement, open posture; eye contact, full listening, speak slower; phone away with people; no oversharing, no gossip, no dominating the room; respond, don't react; simple grooming, fewer better clothes, one fragrance; no validation seeking, no performed confidence. Payoff: calm reads as confidence without a word said.
